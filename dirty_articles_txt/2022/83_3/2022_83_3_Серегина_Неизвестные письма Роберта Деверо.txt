UNKNOWN LETTERS OF ROBERT DEVEREUX,
EARL OF ESSEX, TO KING HENRY IV OF FRANCEFROM
THE PAUL P. DUVROVKY’S COLLECTION
OF MANUSCRIPTS
The publication presents six previously unknown letters from an Elizabethan favourite,
Robert Devereux, Earl of Essex, to King Henry IV of France from the Collation of Paul
P. Dubrovky (St. Petersburg. Russian State Library. Autograph 73. N 7—12). Short letters
are in the Earl’s hand and bear his personal seal. The letters served as a kind of credentials
for the Earl’s messengers or agents who brought the documents to the King of France, to-
gether with sensitive political messages, not trusted to paper. The transcribed letters dem-
onstrate the mode of communications between the English and the French royal courts.
The documents also mentioned the names of couriers or diplomats who could have car-
ried letters and oral messages, which helps reconstruct the scheme of political contacts.

The letters were written in 1591-1596, during the war between France and Spain.
England was an ally of the King of France in this conflict. In the introduction, the author
has reconstructed the political context for the correspondence between the Earl of Essex
and Henry IV of France, and established the circumstances, in which each of the letters
was complied. The letters bear incomplete date — no year is mentioned. The author sought
to establish the date and the place of compilation for each document. The publication
presents the transcriptions of the original letters (in French), their Russian translation,
and commentaries.

Keywords: Early Modern period, England, France, Anglo-Spanish war, war between
France and Spain, political history, Earl of Essex, English court, Henry IV of France,
diplomatic correspondence.
