Summary
of M. A. Barg’s Article «Evolution of Manorial Structure
in the English Midlands, 1086—1279»

New data concerning the manorial structure of feudal landownership in
the 13 th century England required a study of several important retrospect
problems of feudal England’s social history. In particular, it became neces-
sary to tind out, whether the scene described by Е. Kosminsky was a mere
consequence of unchangeable conditions characteristic of 1086, or it appeared
as a result of a deep transformation of these conditions during XII and
XIII centuries. Thus, the question arose, which was the leading line of the evo-
lution of manor in the English village of XI—XIII centuries: integration of

manor, or its desintegration; growth of manor, ог itsdiminution; concentra-
tion of manor, or its dispersion? And in the end: was the prevalence о{ atypi-
cal manorial formations in 1279 an Anglo-Norman inheritance? To what ex-
tent were these atypical manorial formations a result of manor’s disso-
lution?

A systematic comparison of Doomsday Book and Hundred Rolls of 1279,.
made it possible to give the answer based upon mass calculations summing:
up the results of the investigation of tive middle-English shires.

According to the data given in the tables, the period of two centuries
separating the above mentioned sources, was characterized by a mass disin-
tegration of great temporal manor. The number of great manors was reduced
twice (by 43, 4 per cent.), while their quota in the whole number of manors
was reduced almost three times. As far as the middle manor on temporal lands
was not enlarged, it is possible to conclude, that consequences о! the desin-
tegration of great manorial complexes should be found in the sphere of small
manorial formations. The number of small manors in the studied region in-
creased more than twice (from 395 to 926), as well as their arable lands
(from 17,6 per cent. in 1086 to 41,7 per cent. in 1279).

Thus, judging from the réle of great, temporal manor in the feudal landown-
ership, the year of 1086 should be considered the summit of the manoriali-
zation of English Midlands. On the other hand, the process of manoriali-
zation made farther progress in XJI—XII] centuries. But it consisted in
the transformation of former allodial «tenancies» in «typical manors», in.
the enlargement of old ones and even in the formation of new manors on
assarted lands, in the debasement of free tenants and sokemen into a state-
of villainage.

The small, non-typical manor predominant in 1279, was as a whole nei-
ther a reflection of the uncompleted process of feudalization of England, nor:
a «survival» о! the manorial structure of 1086. It should be considered in most
cases a new formation, a result of the above mentioned process of the desin-
tegration of the great manor, i. e. a new event of the English reality of XII —
XIII centuries. It proves utter mobility, instability of those cells, which
till recently had been considered principal unchangable elements of the
structure of feudal landownership, but were in fact strongly influenced by
changing social and economic conditions.
