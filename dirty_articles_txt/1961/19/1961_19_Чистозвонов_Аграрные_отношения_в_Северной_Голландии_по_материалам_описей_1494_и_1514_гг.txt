Summary
of A. N. Chistozvonov’s Article «Agrarian Relations

in North Holland on the Material of Inventories of 1494 and 1514» *.

The main tendencies of the evolution of agrarian relations in North Hol-
land are the same as in South regions, but local teatures are quite clearly
seen. Thus fishery and seataring are widely spread in the villages of Water-
land; the villages of Kennemerland have quite a special juridical status,
preserving a number of mediaeval regional privilleges up to XVI century;
in Western Frisland there are more domanial count’s lands and villages pos-
sessing plattelandsteden, if compared to other regions. Yet some separate
villages of North Holland are characterized by quite a special agrarian struc-
ture. In the villages of Felsen and Heemskerke, in particular, the popu-
lation was juridically divided into two distinct groups of «welgeboren» and
«huisluiden».

The article gives also a consideration of the evolution of agrarian rela-
tions in Holland in the second and third quarters of XVI century.

Summing up the material of the study as a whole, the article reads:
the late XV —early XVI centuries in Holland were characterized by the
development of the process of primary accumulation, by the quickening
of the decay of feudal relations on the basis of wide spreading of commodity
economy and origin of capitalist relations in the town and later in the village.

These events gradually changed the whole of the medieaval system of
agrarian relations and failed all the attempts of the government to prevent
these changes with the help of various legislative limitations. Thus the ag-
таг1ап system of Holland in the considered period represented a transition
from petty-goods economy, gradually relieving itself from feudal ties, to
capitalist farming economy, appearing under the conditions of the progres-
sive process of primary accumulation.
