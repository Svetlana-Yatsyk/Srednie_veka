easily traced. It showed, in particular, in the economic specialization of
various regions, in the fact that a whole number of domestic crafts such as
ship-building, woodwork, weaving, smithwork had turned into a special
and very important occupation of the population of certain settlements and
even districts. At the same time the Swedish bonder were very active in
trade, taking part not only in local but also in long distance and sometimes
even in foreign trade, so that professional merchants appeared in their
midst.

The above mentioned charachter of the economic development of the
Swedish village may help to explain certain specific features of the general
development of the country at that time. Thus, the preservation of the per-
sonal freedom of peasants and steadfast community relations may be partly
considered to be a result of the peasants’ economic independence wrought
by the manifold character of the economy and partly, to be a result of a cer-
tain lack of interest in the intensive growth of private landownership. The
development of peasant handicraft and trade whith the peasants’ personal
freedom preserved, in the 14th and 15th centuries slowed up the growth
of towns and burgherdom, and brought the free, non-shop town handicraft
to domination that lasted up to the 16th century. Yet in the 15th and 16th
centuries the same features facilitated the victory of the manufactury sy-
stem in Sweden.
