ON DIPLOMATIC ОЕ THE VENETIAN LETTERE DUCALI

The charter dated 1355, 149 January, was issued by Marino Faliero, the Doge of Venice.
The Doge orders to Venetian rulers in Laconia (in partibus Sclavonie) to find volunteers
to work on galleys commanded by Brati Vido, Petrus Nanni and Constantinus Zucolo, if
such a necessity will appear. The order is written in form of /ettera aperta and is the most
ancient piece among the Venetian /ettere ducali of the fourteenth to mid-sixteenth centu-
ries, stored in the West-European section in the Historical Archive, St. Petersburg Institute
of History, Russian Academy of Sciences. The set of 48 authentic /ettere ducali drawn by
the sixteen doges of the Republic of Venice is a part of enormous manuscript collection of
academician Nikolay Petrovich Likhachev (1862-1936). The documents in question have
never been published, and went almost unnoticed in the scholarly discourse.
Faliero’s charter was acquired by Likhatchev from the Venetian notary and the custodian
of the notarial archive Gabriele Fantoni (1833-1913), who proposed in 1901 to buy his
collection of ancient documents on the history of Venice. The letters of Fantoni published
by L.G. Klimanov demonstrate that N.P. Likhachev was able both to maintain the seller’s
interest and at the same time to bring down the price of the proposed rarities. Three other
lettere ducali bought from Fantoni are following: 1) the /ettera aperta issued by Fran-
cesco Foscari and dated 1453 is addressed to comes and captain of Shkodra (Scutari)
and other rectors of Albania; it is devoted to villa Grixa granted as a pronoia to magister
Giovanni Murario, 2) the /ейега chiusa issued by Agostino Barbarigo in 1501 and ad-
dressed to Andrea Gritti, than provisor of Asula (prov. Mantua), 3) the /ейега chiusa is-
sued by Andrea Gritti and dated 1524; the Госит tenens and the councilors of Cyprus are
informed that according to the decision Council of Ten they must to release the Bishop
of Naples Paolo Borghese from paying tithes of 130 ducats a year in favor of the camera
of Cyprus.
